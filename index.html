Position

Craft earns the brand. Systems make it compound.

Most marketing organizations rebuild the same campaign over and over. The teams I've led stopped doing that: we built platforms, automated the repeatable work, governed the brand centrally, and measured everything — so creative energy went to the problems that actually deserved it.

AI hasn't changed that thesis — it has accelerated it. The advantage was never knowing more tools than the next marketer. It's holding four disciplines at once: marketing strategy, operational design, executive communication, and change leadership. Tools get commoditized in a quarter. That combination doesn't.

How I make it compound

The five-step method behind every case below

01

See the fragmentation

Find where brand, content, and customer experience have drifted into disconnected efforts — different tools, different owners, no shared system.

02

Design the system

Establish the governance, platform architecture, and ownership model before a single campaign gets built on top of it.

03

Build the experience

Turn the system into the actual work — identity, platforms, content, and the customer- and partner-facing journeys people touch.

04

Mobilize the team

Align leadership, train the people who'll run it, and communicate the change until the new way of working is just the way of working.

05

Measure and refine

Instrument what matters, use the evidence to adjust the system, and let performance — not opinion — decide what changes next.

Case 01Partner Ecosystem PlatformUSLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

A partner portal stewarded for fifteen years — across three roles.

Problem: 200,000+ agents, brokers, and policyholders needed curated business resources the company couldn't produce alone — and third-party content without governance is a brand risk.

Outcome: A durable marketing asset that outlasted campaigns, teams, and platform migrations — the structural blueprint for alliance and ecosystem marketing.

Case 03Digital Broker ExperienceUSLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

A 15-year-old quoting workflow, replaced without losing a broker.

Problem: The broker and agent quoting workspace hadn’t been substantially updated in over fifteen years. A new quoting engine was in development, but no one owned the customer experience, brand, accessibility, or migration strategy for the brokers who’d have to move onto it.

Decision: Direct the portal experience, brand, accessibility, and go-to-market workstreams for USLI-SNAP, partnering with the underwriting and IT teams building the quoting engine itself. Design a migration-first launch — segmented email journeys, webinars, internal training, and employee/product ambassadors — built around awareness, activation, and migration ahead of legacy retirement.

Outcome: Migrated every broker active in the prior two years and secured approval to retire inactive legacy accounts outright. A/B-tested adoption messaging produced an estimated 20%+ lift, delivering a more accessible, intuitive experience without losing the active book of business through the cutover.

Case 06LGBTQ+ Pride ProgramUSLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

Zero LGBTQ+ representation to a company-wide tradition, in three years.

Problem: USLI’s DEI Team was chartered to bring education to the company, but there were no employee resource groups and no LGBTQ+ programming of any kind — no event, ever, had marked Pride.

Decision: Co-propose and co-produce the company’s first-ever Pride program with a fellow DEI Team member, scaling it deliberately year over year: an online trivia and cocktail night to start, then an office trivia event with branch offices joining virtually, building to a flagship year built around a first-ever flag-raising ceremony, a Rainbow Run and Walk 5K held simultaneously at the home office and every branch, a company-wide Lavender Spirit Day with a walk-through educational gallery, and a fireside chat with Traliant on the shifting legal landscape for LGBTQ+ and trans rights.

Outcome: The company went from no LGBTQ+ representation to a visible, well-attended annual tradition with real allyship participation — built as a system from the first year, so it could be carried forward by the community it created, not just by its founders.

2023Launched — first Pride program at USLI
100+Home office 5K/Walk participants, flagship year
4Consecutive years running
Case 07Culture & Inclusion ProgramUSLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

A workforce of 1,500 became the brand’s most credible voice.

Problem: The company’s culture of caring was real but under-told — employee stories surfaced ad hoc, cultural moments were celebrated inconsistently, and engagement depended on individual enthusiasm rather than durable structure.

Decision: Co-found Kaleidoscope with fellow DEI team members in 2024 — an employee activation program built as a system rather than an events calendar: a recurring spotlight storytelling format, cultural programming, a resource library, and a monthly email franchise, with shared ownership designed to run beyond its founders.

Outcome: 188 employee spotlights live within two years, a monthly email read across the company, and adoption now being built into new-hire onboarding to keep the pipeline growing. The program outlived my tenure — which was the design.

About

The Person Behind the Systems

I've spent my career in the space between brand craft and operational systems — a designer by training who kept asking why the work was so hard to produce, and then fixing that instead.

That instinct has an origin: a 1998 co-op at a five-person startup in Paris — rue de Rivoli, across from the Louvre, sharing a floor with Devarrieux-Villaret's Disney, Mercedes, and Swatch teams while I ran press coverage and translation for our own tourism-industry client — where I watched planning, storytelling, visuals, events, and distribution operate as one machine. I've been building versions of that machine ever since — part atelier, part operating system — starting back when websites were hand-coded HTML because there was no other kind.

It carried me through a seventeen-year run at USLI, a Berkshire Hathaway company, rising from Director of Graphics to Second Vice President, Director of Brand Strategy — preceded by global marketing leadership at IDS Scheer during aggressive international M&A, and UX work at Vanguard.

Since 2025, my independent work has focused on AI marketing transformation — the newest chapter in a career-long pattern: master the new capability, then build the system that makes it everyone's.

The through-line outside the org chart is the same as inside it — developing people: mentoring American and French college students and co-workers, teaching photography and serving on the advisory council at Philadelphia's Mercy Career & Technical High School, tutoring young readers, and co-founding workplace programs — USLI Pride and Kaleidoscope — that made the culture more human.

The rest of the time, you'll find me playing fetch with Gumbo, my pocket pit; photographing flowers for a coffee-table book that may never admit it's finished; or on a tennis court.
