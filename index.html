Selected work

Problem / Decision / Outcome

Case 01 · Digital Broker Experience · USLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

A 15-year-old broker/agent quoting platform, replaced without losing an active user.

Problem: The broker and agent workspace hadn't been substantially updated in over fifteen years, and no one owned the customer experience, brand, or migration strategy for the new quoting engine already in development.

Decision: Direct the portal experience, brand, accessibility, and go-to-market for USLI-SNAP, partnering with underwriting and IT on the quoting engine itself. Design a migration-first launch: email journeys, webinars, and internal training built around awareness, activation, and retiring the legacy system.

Outcome: Migrated every broker and agent active in the prior two years and retired inactive legacy accounts outright. A/B-tested messaging produced an estimated 20%+ adoption lift, delivering a more accessible experience without losing the active book of business.

Case 02 · Partner Ecosystem Platform · USLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

A partner portal stewarded for fifteen years, across three roles.

Problem: 200,000+ agents and brokers, and over a million policyholders, needed curated loss control and operating resources the company couldn't produce alone: ungoverned third-party content is a brand risk.

Decision: Design and launch the Business Resource Center as a managed ecosystem: vendor curation, contract negotiation, content governance, and SEO. Then expand it through every role that followed: a Spanish-language edition, a WordPress relaunch, a Canadian expansion.

Outcome: A durable marketing asset that outlasted campaigns, teams, and platform migrations: the structural blueprint for alliance and ecosystem marketing.

175%U.S. usage growth / 18 mo
40%Vendor & resource engagement lift
2Countries, two languages
Case 03 · Brand Repositioning · USLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

Consolidating fragmented channels into one campaign architecture.

Problem: Digital efforts were scattered across channels and teams: each competent, none compounding.

Decision: Lead an enterprise-wide repositioning through a single omni-channel architecture: unified messaging, integrated email journeys, and performance dashboards so every channel reported into one view. Tools: MailChimp, HubSpot, Google Analytics.

Outcome: Engagement and submissions grew because the system did, not because any single campaign got lucky.

460%Digital engagement growth / 1 yr
250%Email-driven submissions
Case 04 · Marketing Infrastructure & Automation · USLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

Replacing manual processes with self-service platforms.

Problem: Marketing delivery depended on IT queues and manual production: a ceiling on speed, innovation, and a $1M+ annual print bill.

Decision: Build the infrastructure instead of the workaround: a white-label marketing platform, automation from the company's first email service provider onward, self-service templates and toolkits for the distribution network, and AI-driven creative tools woven into the workflow for seamless reporting.

Outcome: The team's capacity moved from production to strategy, and delivery got dramatically faster and cheaper.

800%Lead conversion growth
75%Faster delivery times
$1M→$100KAnnual print & promo costs
Case 05 · Global Web Consolidation · IDS Scheer, Consulting & Software

Twenty-four country websites. One governed platform.

Problem: During rapid mergers and acquisitions, 24 self-managed country sites meant 24 versions of the brand, 24 maintenance burdens, and no shared governance.

Decision: Own the roadmap for a global CMS consolidation: aligning international stakeholders with competing priorities, coordinating with the technology partnership team, and leading a team across the U.S. and Germany.

Outcome: One platform, consistent brand and user experience across every market, and sharply lower training and maintenance costs.

24→1Country sites consolidated
GlobalTechnology partnership coordination
Case 06 · Culture & Inclusion Programs · USLI, A Berkshire Hathaway Company

Two employee activation programs, one system: built to run without their founders.

Problem: USLI's DEI Team had no LGBTQ+ programming and no structured way to surface employee stories or culture moments. Both gaps sat on the same small team.

Decision: Co-found two employee activation programs from inside the DEI Team: Pride, the company's first-ever LGBTQ+ program, launched 2023, growing to include a flag-raising ceremony, a 5K, educational resources and events, and a company-wide Spirit Day; and Kaleidoscope, an employee spotlight platform launched 2024, built around storytelling, educational resources, and a monthly email.

Outcome: Over 200 Kaleidoscope employee spotlights published, a monthly email read company-wide, and a Pride tradition that grew to 100+ participants in its flagship year, both built as systems designed to run beyond their founders.

About

The Person Behind the Systems

I've spent my career in the space between brand craft and operational systems: a designer who kept asking why the work was hard to produce, then fixed it.

That instinct started in 1998, at a five-person startup in Paris, watching planning, storytelling, and distribution operate as one machine (part atelier, part operating system).

It carried me through a seventeen-year run at USLI, from Director of Graphics to Second Vice President, Director of Brand Strategy, preceded by global leadership at IDS Scheer and UX work at Vanguard.

Alongside it, I kept building by hand: ten years freelancing in brand identity, web design, and portrait, product, and environmental photography (Tom Pepe Design, 2008 to 2018), then Le Fussy Macaron, a bakery built in partnership, run four years during the pandemic.

Since 2025, my independent work has focused on AI marketing transformation: master the new capability, then build the system that makes it everyone's.

The through-line outside the org chart is the same as inside it, developing people: mentoring students and co-workers, teaching photography, tutoring readers, and co-founding two workplace programs.

The rest of the time, you'll find me playing fetch with Gumbo, my pocket pit; photographing flowers; or on a tennis court.
